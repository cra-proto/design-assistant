About AIDA

The DDPD GenAI Project is exploring how artificial intelligence and automation can improve web content design for Canada.ca.

The AI Design Assistant (AIDA) helps content designers manage projects, analyze pages, identify issues, and create prototypes.

What it does now

  • Generate and manage your content inventory
  • Visualize your information architecture
  • Update your metadata with AI assistance
  • Export your prototypes to GitHub for editing

What's coming next

  • AI-assisted content review
  • Accessibility checking
  • Automated quality assurance tools
  • Have an idea? Contact CDIA.

How AIDA collects its data

When you add a Canada.ca page to your inventory, AIDA automatically fetches information from the live page including the page title (H1), description, keywords, and last modified date.

Page templates

AIDA also identifies which page template is being used. This helps you understand what kind of page you're working with and ensures prototypes use the correct layout. Here's what AIDA looks for:

Navigation pages — Pages where most of the content is links to other pages, like topic pages and index pages. AIDA recognizes:

  • Topic pages (with "Most requested" or service info sections)
  • Pages using the subway navigation pattern
  • Navigation pages (where more than 70% of the text is links)

News and media — Press releases, tax tips, videos, and enforcement notices from the CRA newsroom.

Forms and publications — Form readme pages, guide readme pages, tax guides (like the T1 package), payroll tables, and TD1 forms.

Special page types — Contact pages, campaign pages, PDF download pages, brochures, and video transcript pages.

Content pages — If a page doesn't match any of the patterns above, it's tagged as a standard content page.

Why this matters

Understanding page templates helps you plan your information architecture. For example, if you're moving content from a topic page to a subway navigation page, you'll know the layout needs to change.

If AIDA tags a page incorrectly or if you think we're missing an important template type, please let us know through the Feedback link.
